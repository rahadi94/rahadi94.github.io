About Me

I am a Ph.D. graduate in Operations Management (OM) and Information Systems (IS) at the University of Cologne, and a Visiting Ph.D. Scholar at IE University Business School. My doctoral research was fully funded by a scholarship from the Institute of Energy Economics at the University of Cologne (EWI). My research focuses on applying data-driven and machine learning methods to understand socio-technical systems and enhance efficiency through advanced decision-making models, with a primary interest in sustainable energy and mobility domains. I leverage agent-based modeling, machine learning, mathematical programming, and reinforcement learning to design decision-making algorithms and to understand and predict the impact of user behavior on technology adoption. In my free time, I enjoy advancing my calisthenics skills, hiking, and exploring deep learning architectures, including transformer models and large language models.
